Our History

The Foundation Stone for the church was laid in 1850 by Bishop Augustus Short, the first Church of England Bishop in the colony. The first burial was in August of 1850, but the oldest grave still in existence within our heritage listed cemetery, is that of Mary Davey. Not long after Mary arrived in the colony, Mary and her husband John settled in the area in 1848.

Tragically, Mary died from complications due to childbirth on the 18th of July 1851, and she was buried here on the 21st of July, 1851. It was not until 1851 that the Church was ready for services. The Church has had improvements over the years with the later additions of a Sanctuary and a Vestry.

The cemetery was granted a State Heritage listing in 1985. There are a number of people located in the cemetery that have been awarded the Order of Australia Medal, as well as a number of people that served during World Wars.

There are a number of founding pioneer families interred at the cemetery. Some of the headstones that are found here, are testament to the craftsmanship of the time. The intricate carving and details on these headstones can still be admired today.

Our Vision

We understand how difficult it can be to say goodbye to a loved one. Equally difficult is finding a suitable memorial and location for their remains. We know that the memories of our families who have passed will never be forgotten and we do not necessarily need a place to grieve and reflect, but the Cemetery on the Hill offers you the opportunity to do just that.

At our unique location, you will find a peaceful and beautiful place to mourn your loved ones and to reflect and remember those cherished memories.
